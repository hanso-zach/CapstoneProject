Identifying Threats and Vulnerabilities

Organizations face a wide range of threats and vulnerabilities that must be identified to develop a strong security and disaster recovery plan.

  • Cybersecurity threats: Includes malware, phishing attacks, ransomware, and other digital threats that compromise data and systems.
  • Physical threats: Such as natural disasters (earthquakes, floods), fire, theft, and vandalism that can damage infrastructure and disrupt operations.
  • System vulnerabilities: Software bugs, outdated systems, and unpatched hardware can be exploited, leading to data breaches and service interruptions.
  • FAIR Institute models: The FAIR (Factor Analysis of Information Risk) model provides a structured way to evaluate and quantify risk by analyzing potential loss events, frequency, and magnitude. It serves as a final deliverable in aligning risk with business goals.

Risk Impact Analysis

Risk impact analysis evaluates how different threats may affect the organization both operationally and financially.

  • Probability and impact: Each identified risk is assessed based on the likelihood of its occurrence and the potential impact it could have on operations and assets.
  • Financial and operational consequences: Risks may lead to lost revenue, legal penalties, loss of customer trust, and downtime that severely affects daily functions.
  • Compliance and legal implications: Failing to address risks may result in violations of industry standards and regulations such as GDPR, HIPAA, or ISO 27001, potentially leading to fines and legal action.
