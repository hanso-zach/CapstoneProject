Recovery Objectives and Strategies

  • Recovery Time Objective (RTO) and Recovery Point Objective (RPO): Determine the acceptable downtime and data loss thresholds for each critical system.
  • Critical Business Functions: Identify and prioritize key operations to be restored first during a recovery process.
  • Recovery Sites and Cloud Solutions: Use geographically redundant sites and cloud infrastructure for faster and more reliable recovery.

Business Planning

  • Minimal Disruption: Strategies should ensure essential services remain operational or are quickly restored during and after a crisis.
  • Communication and Coordination: Clear internal and external communication plans must be in place for timely updates and instructions.
  • Training: Employees must be trained on their roles in a disaster situation, including drills and tabletop exercises.

Testing and Maintenance

  • Drills and Simulations: Regularly test disaster recovery procedures through scenario-based exercises to identify gaps.
  • Plan Updates: Frequently review and revise the recovery plan based on technological changes and emerging threats.
  • Continuous Monitoring: Implement systems that provide real-time updates and analytics to assess readiness and response effectiveness.
